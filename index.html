My Favourite Writer

Herman Hesse

Biography

  • 1877 — born on July 2 in Calw, Germany, into a missionary family.
  • 1890 — enters the Maulbronn seminary.
  • 1892 — runs away from the seminary; major conflict with his parents; suicide attempt; psychiatric treatment.
  • 1893 — begins working in a bookshop, which strongly shapes him as a future writer.
  • 1899 — publishes his first poetry collections “Romantic Songs” and “One Hour After Midnight”.
  • 1904 — releases “Peter Camenzind”, gaining early success; marries Maria Bernoulli.
  • 1906 — publishes “Beneath the Wheel”.
  • 1909 — releases “Gertrud”.
  • 1911 — travels to India and Ceylon; this later inspires “Siddhartha”.
  • 1914 — during WWI works for a German aid organization for prisoners of war; writes anti-war texts and gets heavily criticized.
  • 1919 — moves to Ticino, Switzerland; publishes “Demian”, marking a major shift in his style.
  • 1927 — publishes “Steppenwolf”, his most iconic crisis-and-self-search novel.
  • 1931 — marries Ninon Dolbin — the marriage lasts for life.
  • 1932 — publishes “Narcissus and Goldmund”.
  • 1930s — openly opposes Nazism; his works are banned in Germany.
  • 1943 — publishes “The Glass Bead Game”, his most philosophical novel.
  • 1946 — receives the Nobel Prize in Literature.
  • 1950s–1960s — writes essays, paints watercolors, maintains large correspondence; becomes an icon for the counterculture.
  • 1963 — dies on August 9 in Montagnola, Switzerland.
